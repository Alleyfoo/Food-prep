# ADR 0001: Cook and Scout are equal product modes

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-07-12

## Context

Food-prep already provides practical ingredient-transformation and plate-gap
reasoning. Its central research purpose also requires exploring uncommon but
structurally plausible combinations. Treating Scout as a secondary novelty tab
would allow practical Cook work to consume the product direction.

## Decision

Cook and Scout are first-class, equally important product modes built on the
same transformed-state knowledge.

Cook prefers established, contextual, and reliable paths. Scout uses the same
state, sensory, functional, and analogy structures to generate hypotheses that
are worth testing. Scout must keep compatibility separate from corpus novelty,
show uncertainty and risks, and provide a controlled tasting protocol.

The current Streamlit interface is retained as the Workshop for ontology
inspection and curation. Consumer-facing Cook and Scout interfaces will be
built only after their text outputs prove the complete reasoning loop.

## Consequences

- Shared vocabularies and transformed states must support both modes.
- Corpus rarity cannot create culinary compatibility.
- Explanations and rejected hypotheses are part of the data contract.
- Roadmap milestones must advance the Cook and Scout loop rather than only
  expanding ingredient coverage.
- Existing schemas evolve additively until the journey model is proven.
