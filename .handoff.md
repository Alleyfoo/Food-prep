# Handoff — food-prep

## Product direction (decided 2026-07-18)

The focus is a small catalogue of items actually buyable at the user's local
Lidl, turned into interesting foods, balanced food ideas and prepped
components — NOT Nordic cuisine, and not corpus expansion. The catalogue
(pinned in `tests/test_catalogue.py`): broccoli, rutabaga, greens (salad),
corn, tomato, avocado, kale, cucumber, mango, apricot, peach.

Expansion order for full journey treatment (one per session, to the broccoli
quality bar: distinct states, ≥2 destinations, ≥2 flavour routes, risks, ≥1
Scout hypothesis): **rutabaga → cucumber → kale → stone fruit
(peach/apricot/mango)**. Availability stays hand-curated; no scraping until
the catalogue outgrows manual upkeep. The existing classification layers
(roles, availability classes, Cook/Scout, verdicts) are considered sufficient.

## Recommended next task

Rutabaga, cucumber and kale are done — the last item in the agreed expansion
order is **stone fruit (peach/apricot/mango)**, which finishes the
catalogue's deep treatment. Stone fruit is the sweet-savoury crossover
stress test: consider whether one representative fruit (peach) gets the full
tree (raw sliced into savoury contexts / grilled-charred / quick compote or
salsa) while apricot and mango stay fillers, or whether a shared
stone-fruit treatment fits better — decide against the "genuinely new food
state" rule before writing YAML.

Four Scout protocols are now testable with market ingredients: brown butter
on roasted broccoli, apricot in rutabaga mash, peach on salted cucumber,
mango wrapped in massaged kale (`foodprep protocol massaged_kale_component
mango`). Any single recorded verdict via `foodprep record-tasting` unblocks
the "one ingredient, two truths" milestone and the README rewrite.
