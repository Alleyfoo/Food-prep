## Known issues / limitations
- Meal repair only knows the 9 curated component_profiles; unrecognised plate items
  are silently ignored (no honest "I don't have a profile for X" yet — add if scaling).
- Prompt parsing is regex keyword-based, not NLU. "can" guarded against the modal.
- Missing-role logic still hand-curated (intentional per report's "hybrid, not
  learned" stance). CulinaryDB is loaded only as co-occurrence evidence for onion;
  not yet a general corpus backfill.
- Scout pairings are pseudo-Scout (curated), not corpus-rarity-derived.

## Recommended next task
1. **Generalise meal-repair honesty**: when a prompt mentions a food the engine has
   no profile for, say so explicitly and suggest adding a `component_profiles` entry.
2. **CulinaryDB as evidence backfill**: write a loader that reads the 4 CSVs and
   attaches co-occurrence counts to existing pairings (confidence boost / evidence
   rows), rather than only hand-curated. This is the report's "corpus enrichment
   second" step.
3. **Third ingredient for schema stress**: the report suggested onion first (done);
   next pick one that forces a new role family (e.g. **lemon** for acid/heat-via-acid,
   or **cream** for fat/ richness transformations).
4. **Scout from rarity**: derive experimental pairings from low co-occurrence but
   shared-flavour-compound candidates (FlavorDB), instead of hand-authoring.
